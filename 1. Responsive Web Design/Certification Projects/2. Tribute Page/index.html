Arthur Schopenhauer

10 Facts about Schopenhauer

  1. Arthur Schopenhauer was a German philosopher born in 1788.
  2. He is best known for his work "The World as Will and Representation" published in 1818, which is considered one of the most important texts in Western philosophy.
  3. He was heavily influenced by Eastern philosophy, particularly Buddhism, which can be seen in his emphasis on the concept of "will to live" and the denial of the ego.
  4. Schopenhauer believed that the ultimate reality of the world is a blind and irrational will to live, which he called "Will", and that the individual self is an illusion.
  5. He believed that the individual self can achieve true happiness and redemption by denying the will to live and embracing a state of "eternal peace".
  6. Schopenhauer was a strong advocate of compassion and empathy, and he believed that the key to moral behavior is treating others as if they were oneself.
  7. He was also an advocate for women's rights and animal welfare, which is reflected in some of his writings.
  8. Schopenhauer's ideas had a significant influence on later philosophers, including Friedrich Nietzsche and Ludwig Wittgenstein, as well as on literature, art and psychology.
  9. He died on September 21, 1860 in Frankfurt, Germany.
  10. His tombstone bears the inscription "The World as Will and Representation" and is located in the Frankfurt Main Cemetery in Frankfurt, Germany.
